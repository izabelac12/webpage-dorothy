All About Animals

WHAT ARE VERTEBRATES?

Vertabrates are animals that have a backbone.
They have a firm body because of the muscles that connect to their skeleton.
The five most well known classes of vertebrates (animals with backbones)
are mammals, birds, fish, reptiles, amphibians.

WHAT ARE INVERTABRATES?

Invertabrates are animals that DO NOT HAVE BACKBONES.
They have soft inne bodies which held in shape by a flexible
covering of outer cells or by a hard covering called an exoskeleton.

WHAT ARE MAMMALS?

Well there are different characteristics that all mammals have in common such as...

1)Mammals have fur or hair.
2)Mammals are warm-blood
3)The young are fed milk produced by the mother.


BIRDS

Alot of people think that what makes a bird is its wings
but flies have wings so do bees but neither of them are birds.

SO WHAT DEFINES A BIRD?

The answer is feathers!
All birds have feathers, they are the ONLY aninmals that do.


REPTILES

Reptiles are a class of animal with scaly skin.
They are cold blooded and are born on land.
The scales of the reptiles develop as a surface cells filled with Keratin.


AMPHIBIANS

Amphibians are born in the water.
When they are born, they breath with gills like a fish but when they grow up,
they develop lungs and can live on land.


ARTHOPODS

Any animals that have more than four, jointed legs are arthropods such as...
Insects, spiders and crustaceans all belong to this class of animals
